## 0. TL;DR

- **Variant name:** **V4** (`/v4`, code `V4.tsx`).
- **Hero & sticky-collection sidebar** — kept as-is from current V2. They already work.
- **Right-grid surgery:** drop from 11 list+bar tiles to **3 list+bar + 5 bespoke viz + 2 compact**.
- **Two flagships at col-span-2** with tier-hierarchy ratio **2:1** vs compact tiles.
- **Insight layer:** one editorial sentence (≤140 chars, Cormorant 18px italic) under the 3 flagships only. No "AI" labels.
- **Voice:** Hodinkee/Phillips. Russian-watching-Geneva tone.

## 1. Reference deconstruction

### 1.1 Felix-style finance dark
- **Steal:** the **rhythm of one big KPI + a 4-cell mini-row underneath**. We already do this in the hero (`Brands · Novelties · Sources · Countries`). Confirms our ratio choice — keep it.
- **Reject:** purple gradient + dark bg. We're paper-light. Donuts for ranks — already retired in MovementTileF.
- **Replaces:** nothing. Validation only.

### 1.2 Trackify (light)
- **Steal:** the **multi-series crosshair tooltip** (one x-coordinate, three y-values stacked vertically in the tooltip card). Apply to **PriceTileD** — currently it shows count of one bar; should show count + YoY% + share-of-distribution as one stacked tooltip.
- **Replaces:** the in-tile "running tooltip" already there gets enriched, not replaced.

### 1.3 Loud (dark + lime)
- **Steal:** **calendar heatmap** (day × hour). For us → **Brand × Price-bucket heatmap**. This is the single biggest viz win available — we have 61 brands × 11 price buckets, and right now nobody can see "who plays in which segment." Lime accent → swap for our gold.
- **Steal #2:** the editorial H1 register ("Welcome back, Angela"). We already do this with `Novelties` 96px light.
- **Replaces:** **a new tile** (`MarketMapTile`) — promoted to col-span-2 flagship. Pushes either Diameter or Editions out of flagship slot.

### 1.4 NobleFinance (dark + green)
- **Steal:** the **dot-line bubble row** (each row of the chart = one category, dots along the row = sub-items sized by amount). For us → **MaterialsTile** redesign: one row per material, dots along the row = each top brand using that material, sized by count. Solves "Materials is just another bar list" + adds a brand × material cross-cut for free.
- **Replaces:** `MaterialsTileB` (the swatch+bar list). MaterialsTileB demoted to sandbox-only.

### 1.5 Financia (dark + yellow)
- **Steal:** **vertical density-bars per category in the top row** (a row of mini-histograms used as a navigation strip). Could replace the **HeadlineInsights** strip — instead of 5 quote cards, 5 mini-distributions (price, dial, diameter, materials, complications) stacked horizontally as 80px-tall sparkbars. Each functions as a 5-second summary + visual TOC of what's below.
- **Reject:** the dot-matrix kinetic display (looks gimmicky for an editorial report).
- **Replaces:** `HeadlineInsights.tsx` becomes `FairAtAGlance.tsx` — 5 mini-distributions instead of 5 pull-quotes. (Pull-quotes migrate INTO the insight layer under flagships.)

### 1.6 Exon (dark + green) — security ops
- **Steal:** the **AI Insight block with one-paragraph plain-English summary**. But — strip the SaaS framing. No ✨, no "AI Insight" label. We adopt the **structural idea** (one short editor's sentence under the chart) but encode it as the editorial Note (see §4).
- **Steal #2:** the **radial gauge for a single % KPI**. We already have it (MovementTileF dial = 89% mechanical). Confirms direction.
- **Replaces:** informs the insight-layer spec, not a tile.

## 2. Trend research — what to learn

- **Stripe Press / Atlas:** density without clutter via **generous column rules and consistent baseline grid**. Take this: every tile aligns to a 32px row rhythm; vertical hairlines separate adjacent KPIs (we already do this in the hero).
- **Linear Method site:** **monochrome with one accent**, restraint of typography, no decorative chart skins. Take: drop the rainbow `success/error` chips on every single tile — keep colour for the *one* metric per tile that carries the story.
- **Bloomberg Terminal / Launchpad:** **information density on dark with no chrome**. We won't go dark on the canvas, but the *sticky left sidebar* (already chocolate) can carry one Bloomberg-style live ticker — running brand+novelty count update. Defer to v3.
- **Hodinkee:** **photography + one-paragraph context per block, no charts at all**. Take: the **voice** of the insight layer (§4) is Hodinkee, not dashboard. Past tense, watch-trade vocabulary, one human sentence per block.
- **Phillips / Christie's auction reports:** **print-grade typography, all caps eyebrows in a serif italic, lots of white space, single signature image per spread**. Take: the editorial Note typography (Cormorant Garamond italic, see §4) is straight out of the Phillips season-recap PDFs.
- **NYT Upshot / WIRED:** **chart + caption pairing where the caption tells the story and the chart proves it**. Take: insight layer placement must be *adjacent* to its tile (caption-to-image), not floating in a separate panel.

## 4. Insight layer — "Editor's Note"

**Placement:** **inside the tile**, below the viz, above the bottom padding. Hairline `border-t border-ink/10` separates it from the chart. Not floating, not in tile footer — it must read as part of the tile, like a Hodinkee photo caption.

**Which tiles get it (3 only):**
1. `BrandsTileD` — flagship
2. `MarketMapTile` — flagship NEW
3. `PriceTileD` — flagship

CollectionsTileB does *not* get one — it's the visual twin to Brands; one Note covers the pair. MovementsAndComplications and MaterialsBubbles also don't get Notes — they earn their keep through novel viz.

**Voice:** past-tense, watch-trade noun choice ("maison", "novelty", "reference", "tier"), one human observation, no "AI" or "Insight" framing. Treat it as the editor's caption.

**Length cap:** ≤ 140 characters (one line at typical column width on desktop, wraps to two on narrow). Hard cut.

**Typography:**
- Family: **Cormorant Garamond** (already loaded for editorial)
- Style: **italic 18px / leading 1.4 / colour `text-ink/70`** (~ #4F4D49 — softer than body ink, signals "voice" not "data")
- No quote marks, no em-dash prefix, no eyebrow label. Just the sentence.

**Visual marker:** **none**. The font shift (Cormorant italic in a Lato page) IS the marker. A label like "Insight" / "Note" / ✨ would cheapen it. The font carries the meaning.

**Three example sentences for our actual data:**

1. **Under BrandsTileD:**
   > *Rolex held the lead with 58 references, but Tudor closed the gap to under 2× for the first time since 2022.*

2. **Under MarketMapTile:**
   > *The $10–25K corridor is dominated by Rolex and Tudor; everyone above $100K is essentially Patek and a long, quiet tail.*

3. **Under PriceTileD:**
   > *Mid-luxury swallowed the fair: 35% of novelties landed between $5K and $25K, the tightest concentration in five editions.*

**Implementation note:** these are static strings in `data.ts` under `editorNotes: { brands: '...', marketMap: '...', price: '...' }`. No generation, no logic. Editorial copy is content, not output.

## 5. Tier hierarchy — grid math

Current right-grid: `grid-cols-2 gap-4`, all tiles equal `p-6 + rounded-sm + shadow`. Flat.

**Proposal — three explicit tiers, expressed in grid + visual treatment:**

```
grid-cols-12 gap-4

Row 1 (flagship pair — Brands & Collections):
  BrandsTileD       col-span-6   p-7   bg-white   shadow-lg   minHeight: 360px
  CollectionsTileB  col-span-6   p-7   bg-white   shadow-lg   minHeight: 360px
  → these two are the editorial cover. They share the insight Note (one Note, sits under Brands).

Row 2 (flagship NEW — Market Map):
  MarketMapTile     col-span-12  p-7   bg-white   shadow-lg   minHeight: 320px
  → full-width. Single signature viz of the page.

Row 3 (flagship — Price):
  PriceTileD        col-span-12  p-7   bg-white   shadow-lg   minHeight: 280px

Row 4 (mid-tier, 4-up):
  DiameterTileD            col-span-6   p-6   bg-white   shadow-md   minHeight: 220px
  MaterialsTileBubbles     col-span-6   p-6   bg-white   shadow-md   minHeight: 220px

Row 5 (mid-tier, 2-up):
  MovementsAndComplications  col-span-6   p-6   bg-white   shadow-md   minHeight: 240px
  DialsTileF (sausage only)  col-span-6   p-6   bg-white   shadow-md   minHeight: 200px

Row 6 (compact strip):
  HeightsProfileStrip   col-span-4   p-5   bg-paper/60   shadow-sm   minHeight: 120px
  StrapsCompact         col-span-4   p-5   bg-paper/60   shadow-sm   minHeight: 120px
  EditionsTileD         col-span-4   p-5   bg-white      shadow-sm   minHeight: 120px
```

**Visual hierarchy levers:**
1. **Padding:** flagships `p-7` (28px), mid `p-6` (24px), compact `p-5` (20px).
2. **Shadow depth:** flagships `shadow-[0_24px_48px_rgba(0,0,0,0.26)]`, mid existing `shadow-[0_20px_40px_rgba(0,0,0,0.22)]`, compact `shadow-[0_8px_20px_rgba(0,0,0,0.14)]`.
3. **Background tone:** compact tiles use `bg-paper/60` (slightly tinted) to recede vs flagship white.
4. **Header size:** flagships use `text-[18px]` gold uppercase; mid/compact stay at `text-[16px]` (current).
5. **Min-height** enforces ratio without breaking responsive.

**Two flagships visually 1.5–2× larger than compact:** ✓ MarketMapTile (320px tall, 12-col wide) and PriceTileD (280px tall, 12-col wide) vs compact (120px tall, 4-col). ~3.5× area ratio.

## 7. Anti-list — what we explicitly reject

1. **No "AI Insight" / "Smart Summary" / ✨ labels.** The editorial Note must look like editor's writing, not a SaaS feature. Any ML/auto-text framing destroys the Hodinkee voice.
2. **No dark mode for the right grid.** Hero stays chocolate, sticky sidebar stays chocolate, but the tiles stay white-on-paper. We are not Bloomberg Terminal — we are the Phillips season recap.
3. **No neon / lime / electric accents.** Every reference dashboard uses lime-green or electric purple. Our accent is and remains gold #A98155. Don't borrow accent colours, only structural ideas.
4. **No tooltips on tiles that don't need them.** Compact strip (Heights/Straps/Editions) gets static labels. Tooltips only on the three flagships where the data has hidden depth (multi-series tooltip on Price, cross-highlight on Market Map, brand detail on Materials Bubbles).
5. **No fullscreen takeovers / modal drill-downs.** Stays a report, not a dashboard. Sandbox overlay (existing) is for designer-side comparison only and won't be on V4 route.
6. **No redundant chrome.** Every tile gets its identity from one signature element (logo column, swatch column, dial silhouette, heatmap grid). Drop secondary insight blocks ("Steel X vs gold family Y") and right-side sub-metrics — they cheapen the layout.
